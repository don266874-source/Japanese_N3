About Me & My Shadowing Journey

Hey — I'm Felix. I'm not a professor, not a polyglot, and definitely not someone who "always had a gift for languages." I'm just a learner who got tired of studying Japanese the wrong way.

Here's the short version of my story: I moved to Tokyo with big dreams and a stack of JLPT prep books. I could ace the reading sections. I knew my kanji. But the first time I tried to order food at a busy izakaya, I froze. The server spoke at normal speed — and I understood almost nothing.

📸 Tokyo in spring. Beautiful — and humbling.

📸 A local matsuri. Real Japanese, real speed, real confusion.

I lived in Tokyo for a year. I explored neighborhoods, made friends, documented street life. But my spoken Japanese stayed stuck at "survival level." I could read a menu. I couldn't have a real conversation.

The Turning Point

A few months later, I sat down with some local volunteer teachers I'd met — people who had seen me struggle for months. I asked them directly: "What am I doing wrong?"

"Felix, you need to start shadowing. It feels slow. It feels tedious. But it's the fastest path to actually speaking."

They were right. And they were wrong about one thing — it wasn't the fastest path. It was the only path that finally worked for me.

📸 The people who changed everything.

Why I Built This Hub

After leaving Tokyo, I faced a new problem: without daily immersion, my Japanese started fading fast. I needed a way to keep training — not grammar drills, not flashcards, but the actual sound of the language.

So I started building tools for myself. I ripped audio from real podcasts (like Teppei-sensei's beginner series). I wrote code to loop single lines endlessly. I built dual-language transcripts so I could check my understanding without breaking flow.

Then I realized: if I needed this, other learners probably do too.

That's why this site exists. Not as a "teacher's platform" — as a fellow learner's digital war room. Every tool here is something I actually use. Every transcript is something I've shadowed myself.

If you can read Japanese grammar but feel lost when natives actually speak — I've been there. Let's figure this out together.

— Felix
