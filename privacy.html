Privacy Policy — The Shadowing Geek

Last Updated: July 8, 2026

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how The Shadowing Geek ("we", "us", "our") — a personal, independently operated website at https://shadowgeek.net — collects, uses, and shares information about you when you visit our site.

We are a one-person, non-commercial educational project focused on JLPT Japanese grammar and shadowing practice tools. We are not a company, and we do not operate a backend server or database. Our site is a pure static HTML/CSS/JS site deployed on Cloudflare Pages. This matters for your privacy — see Sections 3 and 4 for exactly what that means in practice.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information Automatically Collected by Our Host (Cloudflare Pages)

Because our site is hosted on Cloudflare Pages, Cloudflare's edge network automatically processes certain technical data every time you visit:

  • IP address (visible to Cloudflare; we do not have access to it in a personally identifiable form)
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Referring URL (the page that linked you here)
  • Date, time, and duration of your visit
  • Pages requested

Cloudflare uses this data for network security, DDoS mitigation, and aggregated traffic analytics. We do not receive or store raw access logs, and these logs are not linked to any personally identifiable information we control.

Note: Cloudflare's privacy practices are governed by their own Privacy Policy and Data Processing Addendum. As a Cloudflare Pages customer, we rely on their infrastructure-level protections.

2.2 Information Collected by Third Parties

We do not collect, store, or process the following on our own servers (we have none):

  • Names or email addresses (unless you voluntarily contact us via email — see Section 7)
  • Payment information
  • Account credentials
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers

Third-party services integrated into this site may collect limited data as described in Section 4.

3. Cookies and Similar Technologies

3.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by your browser when you visit a website. They are used to remember your preferences, maintain sessions, and — in the case of advertising cookies — serve content relevant to your interests.

We use the very limited data available to us solely to:

5. Your Rights (GDPR & Applicable Privacy Laws)

Although we are a small personal site, we respect privacy rights under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable laws. You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of any personal data we hold about you (see Section 7 for how)
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Deletion: Request deletion of any personal data we hold about you
  • Restriction: Request restriction of processing
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Data portability: Request your data in a structured, machine-readable format

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email address in Section 7. We will respond within 30 days. Since we maintain no user database or backend, in almost all cases we will have no personal data to disclose — but we will confirm this to you in writing.

Residents of the European Economic Area (EEA): If you are located in the EEA, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority at any time.

Residents of California: Under the CCPA, you have the right to know what personal information is collected, request deletion, and opt out of any sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information.

6. Data Retention

  • Cloudflare logs: Retained by Cloudflare per their data retention policy (typically up to 30 days for security logs; aggregated analytics are anonymized within 12 months).
  • AdSense data: Aggregate impression/click/revenue data is retained per Google's data retention settings (AdSense account-level, configurable). We do not retain individual user-level data from AdSense.
  • Cookies: As described in Section 3 — range from session-length to 24 months depending on the cookie.
